About Us Anything But Ordinary

Anything But Ordinary Productions is a student-run theatre troupe based in Central Bucks.

Our Board

Andrew Cohen

Andrew Michael Cohen's career has run the gamut of theatre, especially while involved in ABO Productions. As a founding member he was both an actor and a director for "A Very Potter Musical". Having to devote his summer to studies in NYC, he wasn't available as a full time consultant for "Where's Waldo: The Musical", but he did help with casting and with the production process by serving as a producer. During the winter, Andrew produced and organized "A Broadway Cabaret Night" which helped to debut ABO Production's horizons. He now studies Music Theatre and Theatre Production at NYU's Steinhardt School.

Billy Ambrose

Billy Ambrose is a Film major at Drexel University and a founding member of ABO Productions. He first co-directed A Very Potter Musical alongside Andrew Cohen and Jack Habib in 2014, and then in 2015 took an integral part in Where's Waldo: The Musical, which he co-wrote and starred in. He has been involved with theater productions all around the Philadelphia area since childhood and now participates in multiple Drexel Co-Op Theater productions and continues to lead ABO Productions into a bright future with his friends.

Nick Goodyear

Nick Goodyear is thrilled that you're looking at this really sweet website. Nick's first involvement with the company was playing the role of Harry in ABO's production of A Very Potter Musical. After a successful year, Nick (with the help of many others) wrote the book and the music for and directed Where's Waldo?: The Musical. He's looking forward to the future of the company. Stay tuned for info on future events!






Ben McLaughlin

Hello! I am Ben McLaughlin and I'm so proud to be a board member of ABO. I have been theater since I was a young kid and have loved growing with these great people. Some of my theatrical highlights include being cast as Evelyn in production of Anything Goes, Lewis in Pippin, and J.B. Biggley in the show How to Succeed in Business without Really Trying. I was an original member of ABO in their first show, A Very Potter Musical in which I played the role of Voldemort. I was also involved in writing directing and acting in the most recent production, Where's Waldo: The Musical. I'm so excited to see where ABO will go and I'm honored to be working with such talented people.

Casey DeMas

Casey is a journalism student at Fordham University and a proud member of the ABO team. She has always participated in the performing arts, by means of singing, acting, and dancing. She has been with ABO since its birth, and is excited to see it grow on the future.

Maria Corr

Maria has always be involved in theater and is thankful that ABO Productions has allowed her to further explore her passion for the arts, both on and off the stage. Her first involvement with the company was as a script editor for Where’s Waldo?: the Musical; she was later cast as Mrs. Odlaw in the stage production. She is now studying English at the University of Notre Dame. As an ABOard member, Maria is so excited for more creative projects with the ABO family in the future!






Nicole Telesco

Nicole is a student at West Chester University studying Early Grades Preparation and Special Education with a minor in Deaf Studies. She has been involved in theater since she was 8 years old, performing in shows such as Cabaret (Frau Kost), Once on this Island (Andrea Deveraux), and Shakespeare’s A Midsummer Night’s Dream (Puck). Nicole first worked with ABO for the first production, A Very Potter Musical (Draco Malfoy). She was extremely honored to have the opportunity to co-direct ABO’s original show, Where’s Waldo?: The Musical, and even more excited to become a member of the board. Nicole is looking forward to participating in future productions with ABO.



Jacob Snively

Jacob Snively is currently studying Theatre at Temple University. His first appearance on stage was during his 9th grade year at Lenape Middle School in Disney’s Camp Rock. Since then he has worked on many shows both through his high school career, and during his time at Temple. Jacob’s first introduction to the ABO family was during A Very Potter Musical where he played the role of Goyle and Rumbleroar. Then in the ABO original show, Where’s Waldo?: The Musical, Jacob acted as lead sound designer and the role of Buff Criminal. As a board member Jacob hopes to see the ABO family grow and continue to do well in all its endeavors.



Trey Hennessy

Trey Hennessy is just one of the colorful cast of characters who make up the student leadership in ABO productions. He is currently studying Secondary Education Social Studies at Kutztown University with minors in History and Political Science. Trey has a different perspective than almost all of the other board members because he is not a performer and has always preferred the technical and production side of theatres. He was involved with many shows including ABO’s Where’s Waldo? And CB South’s Phantom of the Opera. He has recently taken interest in writing with his friend Jack Peters and hopes to showcase their work at a future date.



Pat Heaney

Pat is a student at the Pennsylvania State University. He is pursuing a B.S. in Security and Risk Analysis at the College of Information Sciences and Technology with a focus in Information & Cybersecurity (ICS). He has been involved in the technical side of theater since 2009, working on the crew to do both lights and sound for Honk (2010), Bye Bye Birdie (2011), The Music Man (2012), The Giver (2012), and ABO's own original show Where's Waldo: The Musical (2015). He also was involved in his high school TV club, has played a part in the making of various short films, and takes on other personal projects (which can be found on his website). He currently films for ABO and serves as webmaster. Pat is very excited for the future of ABO.
